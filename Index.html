5 Expenses Sole Traders Often Miss

Are you claiming everything you are entitled to? Many self-employed people miss legitimate costs that could reduce their bill significantly.

Read More

1. Home office costs: If you work from home, you can claim a proportion of your rent, mortgage interest, council tax, electricity and heating based on the space you use exclusively for business.

2. Travel and subsistence: Costs for business journeys, including public transport, mileage allowances for your own vehicle, and reasonable food and drink costs when away overnight or for extended periods.

3. Professional fees: Payments to accountants, tax advisors, or legal professionals directly related to your business activity are fully allowable.

4. Insurance premiums: Professional indemnity, public liability and other business insurance costs are deductible expenses.

5. Training and development: Courses, seminars and materials that maintain or improve the skills you need for your current trade or profession can be claimed.

When Should You Become a Limited Company?

Changing structure can save tax, but it is not right for everyone. Here is how to decide the best time to make the switch.

Read More

There is no fixed rule, but many people consider moving from sole trader to limited company once profits reach a level where the tax savings outweigh the extra administrative responsibilities.

Limited companies pay Corporation Tax on profits at lower rates than higher rates of Income Tax, and you also have more flexibility in how and when you extract money from the business, which can improve tax efficiency.

Other factors include personal liability — operating as a limited company creates a separate legal identity, meaning your personal assets are generally better protected — and how you plan to grow or sell the business in the future.

Key Tips for Property Landlords

Understand finance cost relief, allowable repairs and how to structure your portfolio tax-efficiently.

Read More

Finance cost relief: Mortgage interest and other finance costs are no longer fully deducted from rental income. Instead, you receive a basic rate tax reduction. This can significantly affect the tax payable by higher and additional rate taxpayers.

Repairs vs improvements: You can claim the cost of repairs and maintenance, but not improvements that enhance the property beyond its original condition. Understanding the difference is important to ensure you claim correctly and avoid enquiries.

Portfolio ownership: Depending on your circumstances, holding properties personally or through a company structure can produce very different tax outcomes. This is an area where careful planning delivers real long-term benefit.
