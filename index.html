5 Expenses Sole Traders Often Miss

Are you claiming everything you are entitled to? Many self-employed people miss legitimate costs that could reduce their tax bill significantly.

Commonly missed allowable expenses include: home office costs (rent/mortgage interest, heating, lighting, insurance and repairs for the area used exclusively for business), travel between work locations (but not normal commuting), tools and small equipment, business use of your mobile phone and broadband, professional subscriptions and memberships, and even a proportion of your accountancy fees. Even relatively small amounts add up over a year — claiming correctly can save you hundreds or even thousands of pounds.

When Should You Become a Limited Company?

Changing your business structure can save tax, but it is not right for everyone. Here is how to decide the best time to make the switch.

Generally speaking, once your annual profits reach approximately £30,000–£40,000 per year, operating as a limited company often becomes more tax-efficient than being a sole trader. However, tax is not the only factor — limited companies also offer personal liability protection, can be easier to sell later, and may appear more professional to certain clients. On the other hand, there is more administrative work and cost involved. We help you weigh up all the pros and cons for your specific situation before you decide.
